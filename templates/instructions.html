Instructions

In this experiment, you will attempt to build a spaceship from a variety of parts.

The more good parts you use to build your ship, the better your ship will run, but each part has a certain probability of failing.

When a part fails, it damages how well the ship runs instead of helping the ship run.

Working parts will each increase your flight score by 1 point, but failing parts will each decrease your flight score by 1 point.

Unused parts will not affect your flight score.

Parts fail at random: Sometimes a part will work and other times it will be faulty.

Different parts have different probabilities of failing.

Some parts will fail often, while other parts will fail less often.

You and the other participants in this experiment can build on each others' designs in sequence. When you build your ship, you can observe the ship built by the last participant, and which parts failed on each flight that player's ship took (the last participant could also see the ship of the participant before them).

The red highlighted parts are the parts that failed on that ship's flight. These parts may or may not fail on the ship's next flight, since it is random whether a given part fails on any particular flight.

Your goal will be to choose which parts, if any, to use on your ship.

As long as you aren't the last to play, your actions will then be shown to a future participant.

If a ship you see from the last turn is empty, that player chose not to use any parts on their ship.

If you are the first to play, you will not see any previous ship, and you will be the first to play on every round.
